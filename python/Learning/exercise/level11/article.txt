San Francisco police have apprehended a 20-year-old suspect following an incendiary attack on the residence of Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, in the early hours of Friday morning. The assailant threw a Molotov cocktail at Altman's North Beach property, igniting a perimeter gate before fleeing the scene.
An OpenAI spokeswoman confirmed the incident and revealed that the same individual had previously made threats against the company's San Francisco headquarters. She expressed gratitude for the swift response from law enforcement and emphasized that no injuries occurred during the attack.
Approximately one hour after the initial assault, the suspect appeared outside an OpenAI office building and threatened to burn it down, at which point officers took him into custody. As of Friday afternoon, police declined to comment on the suspect's status or potential charges, citing an ongoing investigation.
Altman has emerged as one of the most prominent figures in artificial intelligence following OpenAI's release of ChatGPT in late 2022, which catalyzed the technology industry's current AI boom and attracted hundreds of billions in annual investment. The company acknowledges that AI advancement may trigger substantial social and economic disruption, particularly through workforce displacement. This week, OpenAI proposed government policies to mitigate potential negative impacts, including job training programs, upskilling initiatives, and increased corporate taxation.
